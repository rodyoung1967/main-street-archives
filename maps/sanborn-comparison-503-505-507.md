# Sanborn Comparison — 503 / 505 / 507 Main Street

This file records observations extracted from the uploaded Sanborn map sheets for the Main Street Archive. It is a working map-reading note, not a final architectural survey.

## High-Level Findings

1. The Sanborn series is now primary map evidence that the Main / 5th commercial frontage existed before the 1919 date reported by later secondary sources.
2. The 1919 date for 503 Main should not be treated as proven original construction without reconciling it against earlier Sanborn footprints.
3. The 1925 sheet clearly shows modern-style address labels along Main including `501`, `503`, `505`, `507`, `509`, `511`, `513`, and later numbers on the same frontage.
4. The 1925 sheet directly addresses the open `501 / 503 / 505` question: there was a labeled `501` at the corner/frontage, with `503` and `505` immediately north/up the Main frontage on the same block.
5. The 1925 and 1950 sheets show 503 and 505 as distinct addresses/occupancies, supporting the archive rule that 503 and 505 should remain separate building/address records even when business history overlaps.
6. The 1950 sheet shows a mid-century commercial pattern at 503/505 consistent with restaurant and billiards/cigars/beer uses.

## 1925 Address / Occupancy Notes

The 1925 sheet is now the key evidence for the `501 / 503 / 505` question.

Working transcription from the Main frontage near 5th:

- `501` — store (`S.`), at/near the 5th/Main corner.
- `503` — restaurant (`Rest.`), with concrete-floor note visible.
- `505` — billiards and cigars.
- `507` — dry goods / clothing / gents furnishing or similar commercial label; exact abbreviation needs careful confirmation.
- `509` / `511` / `513` range — grocery, drugs, billiards/cigars, and I.O.O.F. Hall second floor appear in the same frontage sequence.

This should replace the earlier uncertainty that the archive only knew there was a 1925 reference to `501 / 503 / 505`. The map itself now supports that `501`, `503`, and `505` were all separately labeled in 1925.

## 1950 Address / Occupancy Notes

Working transcription from the revised 1950 Main frontage near 5th:

- `503` — restaurant, with concrete-floor note visible.
- `505` — billiards, cigars, and beer; restaurant wording also appears in or adjacent to the same lower-frontage context.
- `507` — separate store space.
- `509A/B` — restaurant / office or related commercial split; exact reading needs confirmation.
- `511` — store / hall second-floor context nearby.
- `513` and above — additional restaurant, bakery/bake-house, and commercial spaces appear north/up the frontage.

This strengthens the 505 Main tavern/bar-lineage research because 505 was already mapped with billiards/cigars/beer use by the 1950 Sanborn revision.

## Construction-Date Implication

The Sanborn maps do not by themselves disprove a 1919 construction or major-rebuild date for the present 503 building, but they do show that the relevant Main-frontage commercial block existed before 1919.

Archive handling:

- Keep the 1919 date as a working hypothesis for 503 Main.
- Do not describe 1919 as proven original first construction.
- Add a new research question: what changed between the pre-1919 Sanborn footprints and the later 1925 address/footprint pattern?
- Check assessor records, permits, deeds, and tax cards for a 1919 construction, remodel, rebuild, readdressing, or reassessment event.
