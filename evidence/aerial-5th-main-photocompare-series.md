# Aerial Photocompare Series — 5th & Main Street

Working analysis of the Oregon City photocompare aerial screenshot series (`S-023`), cataloged as `E-021` with media IDs `IMG-0006` through `IMG-0043`.

## Source and Scope

- **Source:** Oregon City historical aerial photocompare tool — [maps.orcity.org/galleries/photocompare](https://maps.orcity.org/galleries/photocompare/index.html) (`S-023`)
- **View center:** 5th Street and Main Street, Oregon City
- **Coverage:** One screenshot per available year, 1929–2025 (38 images)
- **Repository files:** `media/photos/aerials/` — see `media/photos/aerials/README.md`
- **Related buildings:** `B-001` (503 Main), `B-002` (505 Main), `B-003` (507 Main / Harding Building)
- **Related prior evidence:** `E-020` / Sanborn comparison — especially the 1925 `501` label at the **5th & Main** corner

## Method and Limits

These are **screenshots** from a city-hosted comparison viewer, not original survey negatives. Labels such as "McLoughlin Blvd" and "US99E" appear on some frames and help orientation, but:

- Early black-and-white frames have limited resolution; individual address footprints on the west side of Main are hard to separate confidently.
- **501 Main** on the 1925 Sanborn is at the **5th & Main corner**, not the 6th & Main corner. Aerial interpretation must use that corner, not a different intersection.
- This series supports **footprint and corridor-change observations**; it does **not** by itself prove business names, ownership, or exact demolition dates.

Confidence is **moderate** for highway/corridor labeling and general block continuity; **low to moderate** for isolating 501 Main as a distinct structure across early aerial years.

## McLoughlin Boulevard / US99E

| Period | Observation | Confidence |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1929 (`IMG-0006`) | McLoughlin Blvd / US99E labeling visible west of Main; Main Street commercial frontage still reads as a continuous row between 5th and the river/highway side. | Moderate |
| 1936 (`IMG-0007`) | Highway corridor west of Main appears wider and more graded than 1929; Main-frontage buildings still present along the block. | Moderate |
| 1940–1980 (`IMG-0008`–`IMG-0014`) | Main Street structures between 5th and McLoughlin remain visible in most years; highway and buildings **coexist** rather than showing an obvious "all buildings gone" moment in this view alone. | Moderate |
| 1996 Flood (`IMG-0015`) | High water visible near riverfront / McLoughlin corridor; useful for flood context, not for 501 disposition. | Moderate |
| 2005 Leaf Off (`IMG-0022`) | Leaf-off frame improves roofline reading for the block; still difficult to prove separate 501 vs 503/505 footprints without ground truth. | Moderate |
| 2012–2025 (`IMG-0029`–`IMG-0043`) | Color aerials show current commercial block including 503/505/507 context; replacement Harding Building footprint at 507 readable in later decades. | Moderate to high for current footprint |

**Working conclusion:** The aerial series shows McLoughlin / US99E as an established labeled corridor by **1929**, with a visibly wider graded corridor by **1936**. It does **not** establish a single clear year when US99E construction removed or absorbed the Sanborn-labeled **501 Main** space. That question remains open (`OQ-023`).

## 501 Main — Appear / Disappear Question

Cross-reference: `E-020` and `maps/sanborn-comparison-503-505-507.md` place **501** at the **5th & Main** corner on the 1925 Sanborn sheet.

| Question | Aerial finding | Status |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Can 501 be isolated in 1929–1944? | West side of Main shows continuous roof/footprint mass; separate 501 bay not confidently distinguishable from 503/505 row in B&W frames. | **Unresolved** |
| When does 501 "disappear"? | No confident disappear year from this series alone; block frontage remains built through mid-century frames. | **Unresolved** |
| Did US99E take 501? | Highway widens west of Main, but buildings along Main persist in many post-1936 frames — no simple "highway replaced 501" story visible here. | **Unresolved** — needs deeds, assessor sketches, highway project records |

**Working conclusion:** The aerial series **does not answer** OQ-005's remaining disposition question. It **partially supports** that the Main Street commercial frontage remained built through much of the mid-20th century, which argues against an early-total-clearance interpretation unless 501 merged physically into adjacent addresses.

## 503 / 505 / 507 Block Observations

- **503 & 505:** Appear as part of the continuous Main frontage north from the 5th corner in most years; finer legal/physical boundary not readable from aerials alone (`OQ-004` unchanged).
- **507 / Harding Building:** Taller mass on the north end of the immediate block in earlier frames; post-fire replacement footprint consistent with late-1960s fire narrative (`E-016`, `E-017`) in later color years — exact fire-year confirmation still needs newspapers / fire records.
- **Circa 2017 modification:** Time Capsule Maps metadata (`E-015`) reports significant 503 modification around 2017; 2017+ color aerials (`IMG-0035` onward) may support future permit/photo comparison but are not transcribed here frame-by-frame.
