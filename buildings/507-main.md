## Known / Working History

507 Main Street is associated with the Harding Building and the broader 503-507 Main Street block history.

Current working facts and leads:

- The building was known as the Harding Building by 1909.
- George A. Harding and Harding Drug Store are associated with the building.
- Dr. Bailey was documented upstairs in the Harding Building in 1909.
- Tony's Restaurant and a bakery are associated with the building before the late-1960s fire.
- The original/taller Harding Building reportedly burned in the late 1960s, likely late 1968 or 1969.
- The current replacement building is understood to date to about 1968, but the exact fire date, replacement date, and construction sequence still need primary confirmation.

## Fire / Replacement Building Question

The archive should treat the Harding Building fire as a high-priority research question. Family/project notes suggest the fire may have started in or spread through the bakery area and destroyed the structure. This remains a working claim pending confirmation from newspapers, fire department records, permits, insurance records, assessor records, or city files.

## Relationship to 503 and 505

507 Main should remain a separate building record from 503 and 505. However, historic images of the 5th/Main intersection and the block should be cross-referenced here when they show the taller Harding Building or replacement structure.

## Evidence Summary

- `E-016` - Project/oral-history notes: Harding Building fire in late 1968 or 1969; Tony's Restaurant and bakery involved; bakery area may have been where the fire started or spread.
- `E-017` - Photo metadata note: 1910 5th/Main photo record states the taller Harding Building at 507 burned and was replaced by the current building.

## Research Priorities

- Locate the exact newspaper article or fire report for the Harding Building fire.
- Search Oregon City Enterprise-Courier issues from late 1968 through 1969 manually or via archive access.
- Search city permit records for demolition, fire damage, and replacement construction.
- Search assessor records for a sudden building-value change or replacement date.
- Clarify whether the replacement building was completed in 1968 or after a late-1968/1969 fire.
- Confirm the bakery name and its exact location within or next to the Harding Building.
- Confirm Tony's Restaurant dates at 507 Main.

## Open Questions

- What was the exact date of the Harding Building fire?
- Did the fire start in the bakery area, or did it only spread through that area?
- Was Tony's Restaurant operating at the time of the fire?
- Was the current 507 Main building constructed in 1968, 1969, or another year?
- What permits, assessor cards, or newspaper notices document the demolition and replacement?
