## Known / Working History

505 Main Street is associated with The Wheel / 505 business context, 505 Tavern, and later Thirsty Duck. The building is also tied to a partly unresolved ownership and operations sequence involving Ray Hansen, Ray Grisham, Dick, Sheila, Willie, Victor, Mitch Young, Mike Berman, and Scott Forvilly.

Rodney Young currently owns 505 Main Street and is stewarding this archive. The broader family history connects the Young, Grisham, and Wiitanen names to the 503/505 Main Street property history.

The exact relationship between The Wheel, The 505, 505 Tavern, and the 503/505 Main address boundary still needs confirmation. The archive should continue to preserve 505 Main and 503 Main as separate building records while acknowledging that some business history may involve the combined 503/505 Main context.

## Business Ownership / Operation

- `E-005` - Ray Hansen owned the 505 business before Dick and Sheila. This is currently family/oral history and needs documentary confirmation.
- `E-006` - Willie, a heavyset bartender, worked under Ray Hansen and continued under Dick and Sheila. His full name is not yet known.
- Dick and Sheila owned or operated the business after Ray Hansen. Their full names and exact dates are not yet established in the archive.
- `E-007` - Victor independently managed the restaurant during Ray Grisham's years and early Dick and Sheila years. Victor's full name and exact role need confirmation.
- `E-004` / `OH-001` - Mitch Young owned and operated the 505 Main Street business from March 1991 to March 2004, confirmed directly by Mitch. The exact legal/trade name for that period needs documentary confirmation and should not automatically be equated with The Wheel.
- `E-008` - Mike Berman was a later owner and sold the business in 2017.
- `E-008` - Scott Forvilly purchased the business in 2017 and reopened it as Thirsty Duck.

## Physical Building / Footprint Notes

The rear portion of the 505 building may have been added later, based on family/oral history attributed to Mitch Young. This needs confirmation through permits, assessor sketches, Sanborn maps, tax records, deeds, or physical building analysis.

There is also an unresolved footprint question: 505 may extend farther back than 503, while 503 may be somewhat wider. This should be treated as a working observation until confirmed by measurements, tax maps, assessor records, Sanborn maps, deeds, and parcel records.
